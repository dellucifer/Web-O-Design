India has seen a significant growth in the number of entrepreneurs over the past few years. Here are some statistics on the growth of entrepreneurship in India:

According to a report by NASSCOM, India has the third-largest number of startups in the world, with over 50,000 startups as of 2021.

The number of Indian startups has grown at a CAGR of 12-15% in the last five years.

As per a report by the Economic Survey of India 2020-21, the number of women entrepreneurs in India has increased from 14% in 2014 to 20% in 2019.

According to the Global Entrepreneurship Index 2020, India ranks 43rd in the world, up from 68th in 2014.

The Indian government has launched various initiatives to support entrepreneurship, including Startup India, Atal Innovation Mission, and Stand-Up India.

As per the 'Economic Times' report, India has 100+ unicorns with a total valuation of more than $240 billion.

According to a survey conducted by the National Sample Survey Organization (NSSO), the number of self-employed people in India has grown from 52.2 million in 2011-12 to 57.4 million in 2018-19.

Overall, entrepreneurship in India is growing rapidly, with the government and private sector providing various support mechanisms to encourage and nurture startups.
