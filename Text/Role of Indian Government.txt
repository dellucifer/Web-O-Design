The Indian government has launched several schemes and initiatives to promote entrepreneurship and support startup companies. Some of the notable schemes for entrepreneurs are:

Startup India: This is a flagship initiative launched by the government in 2016 to promote the growth of startups in India. It provides a host of benefits and incentives to startups, including tax exemptions, funding support, and legal assistance.

Atal Innovation Mission (AIM): This is a government scheme launched in 2016 to promote innovation and entrepreneurship among students, entrepreneurs, and startups. It provides support in the form of funding, incubation, and mentoring.

Stand-Up India: This is a government scheme launched in 2016 to promote entrepreneurship among women and marginalized communities. It provides loans to entrepreneurs from these communities to set up their businesses.

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY): This is a government scheme launched in 2015 to provide loans to micro and small enterprises. The loans are provided through various financial institutions such as banks, NBFCs, and MFIs.

National Small Industries Corporation (NSIC) Single Point Registration Scheme: This is a government scheme launched in 1955 to provide a single point registration for micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs). It provides various benefits to MSMEs such as exemption from payment of Earnest Money Deposit (EMD) and waiver of security deposit.

Technology Business Incubators (TBIs): This is a government scheme launched in 2000 to support the development of technology-based startups. It provides support in the form of funding, mentorship, and incubation facilities.

These schemes and initiatives have been instrumental in promoting entrepreneurship and supporting startup companies in India.
